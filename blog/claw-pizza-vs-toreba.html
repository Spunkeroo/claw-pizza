Platform Overviews

Toreba

Toreba is operated by CyberStep, Inc., a publicly traded Japanese company (Tokyo Stock Exchange: 3810). Launched in 2012, it is the oldest and most established online claw machine platform. Toreba connects players to real, physical crane machines located in warehouses in Japan. Players watch a live video stream and control the machine remotely in real time. The platform is available as a mobile app on iOS and Android, as well as through a web browser. Toreba primarily features Japanese merchandise including anime figures, plush toys, snacks, and electronics.

Clawee

Clawee was founded in 2018 by Gigantic, an Israeli technology company. Like Toreba, Clawee uses real physical claw machines that players operate remotely via live video. The platform is primarily mobile-focused with apps on iOS and Android. Clawee differentiates itself with a broader range of international prizes including electronics, fashion accessories, toys, and gift cards. The company has raised over $14 million in venture capital funding and operates machines in multiple countries.

claw.pizza

claw.pizza takes a fundamentally different approach. Instead of remote-controlling physical machines, claw.pizza uses a virtual claw machine system powered by a provably fair algorithm. Every play outcome is determined by cryptographic hashing (HMAC-SHA256) that neither the platform nor the player can manipulate. Prizes include digital collectibles, Bitcoin ordinal inscriptions, and merchandise. The platform requires no app download and works entirely in the browser.

Toreba: The Japanese Pioneer

Toreba's biggest strength is authenticity. You are controlling a real machine, watching a real claw descend onto real prizes, all streaming live from Japan. For fans of Japanese pop culture, the prize selection is unmatched. You will find limited-edition anime figures from series like One Piece, Dragon Ball, and Jujutsu Kaisen, along with exclusive plush toys, Japanese snacks, and branded merchandise that is difficult or impossible to find outside Japan.

Pros

  • Real physical machines
  • Authentic Japanese prizes
  • Large machine selection
  • Free first shipping monthly
  • Established reputation

Cons

  • Expensive ($2-3 per play)
  • Slow shipping (2-4 weeks)
  • No fairness verification
  • Queue wait times
  • Grip strength cycling

The critical issue with Toreba is the same problem all physical claw machines have: the grip strength is programmed to ensure profitability. Toreba's machines use the same payout cycling as arcade machines, meaning most of your plays are statistically predetermined to fail regardless of your skill. At $2 to $3 per play, the cost adds up quickly, and there is no way to verify whether the machine was in a payout cycle during your turn.

Clawee: The Mobile Contender

Clawee offers a polished mobile experience with a wider variety of international prizes than Toreba. The app is well-designed and makes it easy to browse machines, watch other players, and jump into games quickly. Prize categories span electronics, toys, fashion, home goods, and gift cards, giving it broader appeal than Toreba's Japanese-focused inventory.

Pros

  • Diverse prize selection
  • Polished mobile app
  • International prizes
  • Lower cost than Toreba
  • Spectator mode

Cons

  • Still $1-2 per play
  • Slow shipping (2-6 weeks)
  • No fairness verification
  • Mobile app required
  • Shipping costs add up

Clawee suffers from the same fundamental transparency problem as Toreba. Players have no way to verify win rates or confirm that the machines are operating fairly. Community forums frequently feature complaints about machines that appear to drop prizes at the last second, a hallmark of grip strength cycling. Combined with shipping costs that can range from $5 to $15 per prize, the total cost of winning can be substantial.

For players who value digital collectibles, Bitcoin ordinals, and blockchain-verified prizes, claw.pizza offers a prize category that the other platforms simply do not have. And with free daily plays, there is zero barrier to entry.
