The Rise of Online Claw Machines

The concept of playing a claw machine remotely is not entirely new. Toreba, a Japanese company owned by CyberStep, launched its online crane game service back in 2012, allowing players worldwide to control real physical claw machines via live video streams. The idea was revolutionary: players could win authentic Japanese merchandise, anime figures, and plush toys without ever setting foot in a Tokyo arcade.

By 2024, the online claw machine market had grown significantly. Mobile apps like Clawee and ClawBuster brought the experience to smartphones, while newer platforms began experimenting with virtual claw machines that used random number generators instead of physical hardware. This shift opened up new possibilities for fairness, speed, and prize variety.

In 2026, the market is more diverse than ever. Players can choose between physical remote-controlled machines, fully virtual experiences, and hybrid platforms that blend both approaches. The best part? Many platforms now offer free plays, making it possible to win real prizes without spending a cent.

Top Online Claw Machine Platforms in 2026

Toreba

Toreba remains the most well-known online claw machine platform globally. Operated by CyberStep, Inc. out of Tokyo, it features real physical crane machines that players control through live video feeds. The platform is famous for its anime merchandise, plush toys, and Japanese snacks. Toreba offers free plays to new users and periodically through daily bonuses, though regular play costs roughly $2 to $3 per attempt. Shipping is free for your first prize each month from Japan, with additional prizes incurring a shipping fee. The main drawbacks are slow international shipping times (often 2 to 4 weeks) and the fact that physical machines can be unpredictable.

Clawee

Clawee is a mobile-first platform available on iOS and Android that features real claw machines controlled remotely. Founded in Israel, the app has built a large user base with its polished interface and variety of prizes including electronics, toys, and gift cards. Plays typically cost between $1 and $2 each, with occasional free play promotions. Clawee ships prizes internationally, though delivery can take 2 to 6 weeks depending on your location. The platform does not provide any transparency into its win rate algorithms or claw strength settings.

ClawBuster

ClawBuster is a newer entrant that operates virtual claw machines rather than physical ones. This means results are generated by software rather than a real mechanical claw. The platform offers a variety of prize tiers and accepts both traditional payment and cryptocurrency. Plays start at around $0.50, making it one of the more affordable options. However, ClawBuster has faced community criticism over unclear win rate disclosures and a lack of independent verification of its random number generation.

What Makes a Good Online Claw Machine

Not all online claw machines are created equal. When evaluating a platform, there are several critical factors to consider.

Fairness and Transparency

The single most important factor is whether the platform is fair. Traditional physical claw machines are well-documented to adjust grip strength based on profitability targets. Many online platforms inherit this same lack of transparency. A truly fair platform should either disclose its win rates openly or, better yet, use a provably fair system that allows players to verify every outcome independently.

Prize Quality and Variety

Good platforms offer prizes that are actually desirable and match what is advertised. Be wary of platforms where the prize images look significantly better than what winners actually receive. Look for platforms that show real winner testimonials and unboxing content.

Cost and Value

Consider the cost per play relative to the prize value. If a platform charges $2 per play and the advertised prize retails for $20, you need to understand the actual win rate to calculate whether it is a good deal. Platforms offering free plays give you the chance to evaluate the experience without financial risk.

Speed and Convenience

Physical remote-controlled machines require waiting for your turn, dealing with lag, and waiting weeks for shipping. Virtual platforms with instant results and digital prize delivery eliminate these friction points entirely.
